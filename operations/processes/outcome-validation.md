# Framework outcome validation

Outcome validation asks whether the framework helps its consumers define,
inspect, operate, validate, and improve complete adaptive problem-solving
systems. Internal consistency alone cannot establish this.

Use one or more of these methods, proportionate to the claim:

- apply the compilation process to retained historical source evidence in a
  clean temporary context, complete its proposed output, and compare it with the
  hidden historical output;
- apply the framework to a real system and record gaps it exposes;
- create or revise an implementation example and observe where the framework
  is insufficient, ambiguous, or burdensome;
- ask a consumer to define or assess a system without unstated maintainer help;
- compare decisions or results before and after an APSS-guided adaptation; or
- revisit users after operation to see whether the defined learning loop
  actually changed later work.

Record the consumer and context, attempted outcome, observation period,
evidence, confounders, failures, and conclusion. Distinguish:

- evidence that the artifact was understandable;
- evidence that a complete system could be declared;
- evidence that the declared system operated as described; and
- evidence that adaptation improved a later result.

If outcome evidence is pending, say so in the work log and avoid describing the
change as proven effective. Delayed outcome validation may close a work item
after artifact release or reopen the strategy when results contradict it.
