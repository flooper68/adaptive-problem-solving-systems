# APSS connects evidence, deliberate interpretation, action, and learning

## Insight

### Claim

APSS can be understood as a recurring flow in which streams provide evidence;
deliberate processes or working sessions interpret and shape that evidence;
selected responses such as research, discussion, experiments, or production
work enter planning and execution; and learning or compilation turns the
resulting evidence into artifacts that inform later adaptation.

### Scope

This is a candidate high-level mental model of the APSS framework. It may help
explain how streams, uncertainty resolution, planning, execution, compilation,
artifacts, and adaptation relate. It is not yet established as a normative
framework concept, ordering constraint, or claim that every process is a
working session.

### Reasoning

The maintainer observed that much of APSS consists of obtaining information,
digesting it through purposeful interaction or processing, selecting resulting
actions, and compiling what is learned into useful outputs. Treating these as a
connected flow may explain the framework more clearly than presenting each
responsibility only as a separate mechanism.

The proposed model also suggests that research, discussion, grooming, and
compilation may share a broader session-like shape: each is invoked for a
purpose, consumes context or evidence, follows a process, and produces a
retained result. Whether that common shape warrants a new high-level concept is
the central unresolved inference.

## Evidence

### Supporting

- The maintainer proposed the model in working session
  `apss.session.records-and-work` while distinguishing retained records from
  executable work.
- The normative APSS loop already connects evidence streams, discussion,
  research, experimentation, planning, execution, artifact production,
  knowledge compilation, and adaptation.
- The framework visualization already names a “validated insight” as an
  outcome-learning artifact, indicating that APSS expects some interpretations
  to become assessed learning even though it does not define their lifecycle.
- The operations working-session process demonstrates one bounded form of
  deliberate interpretation that consumes evidence and produces routed
  outcomes.

### Contradictory or limiting

- The normative loop currently treats discussion, research, experimentation,
  execution, and compilation as distinct capabilities or responsibilities, not
  instances of one named working-session concept.
- Research and discussion may resolve uncertainty before work selection, but
  they may also be deliberately planned actions; a single linear ordering may
  obscure that iteration.
- Systems produce primary artifacts through execution as well as compiled
  knowledge through learning. Compilation is therefore not the only
  artifact-producing activity.
- No consumer evidence yet shows that this higher-level model improves APSS
  understanding or application.

## Possible implications

- The framework may benefit from a general concept for bounded, purposeful
  evidence-processing sessions, with meetings and grooming as possible forms.
- A future framework explanation or visualization might show evidence intake,
  interpretation, action selection, artifact production, learning, and
  adaptation as a recurring flow.
- Existing processes may share a reusable session contract without erasing
  their distinct evidence standards, authorities, and outputs.
- Any normative response should follow compilation and adaptation rather than
  being inferred directly from this captured insight.

## Open questions

- Is a “working session” intended as an umbrella for any bounded knowledge-work
  activity, including research and compilation, or specifically collaborative
  deliberation that may invoke those processes?
- Which inputs, process properties, outputs, and stopping conditions are truly
  common across meetings, grooming, research, experiments, execution, and
  compilation?
- How should the model represent iteration when interpretation can precede
  planning, be planned itself, or occur during execution?
- Which artifact is produced at each stage, and which artifacts are intended
  for consumers versus future system learning?
- What consumer test would show that the proposed model improves understanding
  rather than merely adding terminology?
- Does a scoped `supported` disposition sufficiently represent a “validated
  insight,” or should validation remain another explicit linked result?
