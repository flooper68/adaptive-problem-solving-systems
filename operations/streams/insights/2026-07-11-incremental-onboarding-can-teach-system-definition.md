# Incremental onboarding can teach APSS through system definition

## Insight

### Claim

APSS may be easier to share with other people if it provides an onboarding flow
that helps them define their own systems while teaching the framework through
the activity. The flow does not need to produce a complete system definition to
be useful: helping someone take any small, meaningful step toward understanding
or defining a system may be a worthwhile outcome.

### Scope

This claim concerns the first experience of people being introduced to APSS and
attempting to apply it to their own context. It supports an incremental form of
onboarding in which a participant might clarify one problem, boundary, artifact,
role, or other system concern without completing the full declaration.

The claim does not yet specify an audience, delivery medium, required sequence,
or minimum completed artifact. It also does not establish that onboarding must
be a separate tool rather than a guided process, conversation, exercise, or
layer over the existing system-definition materials.

### Reasoning

Sharing a framework asks a newcomer to understand its concepts and translate
their own situation into those concepts at the same time. A guided flow could
turn that translation into the teaching method: each prompt introduces a
concept by helping the participant use it on a real system.

Allowing partial progress may reduce the threshold for engaging with APSS. The
participant could gain useful clarity or learn a framework concept even when
they are not ready to create a complete, valid system declaration. This is a
prospective interpretation, not yet a conclusion drawn from observed users.

## Evidence

### Supporting

- The maintainer proposed an “onboarding flow / process” that helps people
  define their systems as both an onboarding tool and a teaching tool.
- The idea arose while the maintainer was thinking about sharing APSS with
  others, identifying first contact and transfer to new users as the relevant
  context.
- The maintainer clarified that “any small step to help is enough,” supporting
  incremental progress rather than requiring a completed system definition.
- The existing task `apss.system-definition-guide` observes that APSS has a
  template, schema, checklist, and completed example but no guided walkthrough
  from an ambiguous situation to a reviewable definition.

### Contradictory or limiting

- No prospective user has yet attempted the current materials or reported an
  onboarding difficulty.
- No evidence yet shows that guided definition teaches APSS more effectively
  than documentation, examples, conversation, or another learning format.
- Accepting any small step creates a deliberately low success threshold; later
  work would still need to distinguish meaningful progress from an interaction
  that feels helpful but does not improve understanding or system definition.
- Different audiences may need different starting points, terminology, depth,
  and forms of guidance.

## Possible implications

- The system-definition guide could be designed as progressive onboarding that
  teaches concepts at the moment a participant applies them.
- The flow could produce useful intermediate artifacts instead of requiring a
  complete `SYSTEM.md` in one session.
- Onboarding could offer multiple entry points, such as clarifying the problem,
  testing the system boundary, or naming the primary artifact.
- Early validation could look for one observable improvement in a participant's
  understanding or definition rather than successful completion of the entire
  framework workflow.
- Any executable response remains in the related system-definition-guide task
  and should not be inferred merely from this captured insight.

## Open questions

- Who is the first intended audience, and what prior knowledge can be assumed?
- Which small steps are independently useful and safe to teach without the rest
  of the system definition?
- Should onboarding follow one recommended sequence or let a participant enter
  through the concern that is currently most useful to them?
- What delivery form would best combine guidance and teaching: documentation,
  an interactive tool, an agent-led conversation, a workshop, or something
  else?
- What observable change would show that one onboarding step helped?

## Intake and clarification record

### 2026-07-11 — captured

The APSS framework maintainer proposed adding an insight that it would be useful
to have an “onboarding flow / process” which helps people define their systems
as an onboarding and teaching tool. The initial wording is preserved here as a
prospective claim rather than evidence of demonstrated user need.

The first clarification asked whether a newcomer should leave with a real,
usable definition or mainly enough understanding to define it afterward. The
maintainer answered that “any small step to help is enough.” The claim and scope
were updated to allow useful partial progress rather than requiring a complete
definition or curriculum.

The second clarification asked what in the current materials prompted the idea.
The maintainer explained that they were thinking about sharing APSS with others.
This establishes the anticipated sharing context while leaving the actual
audience and its needs open. No usability failure or consumer evidence was
inferred.

The clarification grill stops after these two load-bearing questions. The
incremental outcome and sharing context are clear enough for capture, while the
audience, medium, sequence, and success evidence remain appropriate questions
for grooming or a future consumer trial. The maintainer participated through
this stopping point, which approves delivery of the bounded insight record
under the insight-intake process. It does not approve the insight's truth,
